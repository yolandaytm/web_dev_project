Topic 5: 하오체

Semi Formal / Old Style Polite

When to Use?

  • Historical dramas and period films
  • Confucian-style formal speech
  • Traditional ceremonies and rituals
  • Elderly people in certain regions
  • Classical literature and historical texts
  • Official letters in traditional format
  • Jeolla and Chungcheong regional dialects
  • Poetry and traditional performances
  • Rare in modern Seoul Korean speech
Regional Note: While rare in modern Seoul Korean, 하오체 is still occasionally heard in Jeolla and Chungcheong regions among older generations.

Verb Endings

Type Ending Example English
Statement -오
갑오
(go + 오)
 I go (polite old style)
Question -오?
어디 가오?
(where go + 오?)
 Where are you going?
Command -소 / -시오
가시오 / 들오소
(go + 시오 / listen + 오소)
 Please go / Please listen
Suggestion -다오 / -시오
함께 합시다오
(together do + 시다오)
 Let's do together (old tone)
Note: 하오체 does NOT use -습니다 or -ㅂ니다 endings. Those belong to 합니다체.

Historical Drama Dialogue:
양반
이리 오시오. 자리에 앉으시오.
Come here. Please take a seat.
손님
감사하오. 이 자리 좋소.
Thank you. This seat is good.
양반
차 한 잔 드시겠소?
Would you like a cup of tea?
손님
네, 사양치 않겠소.
Yes, I won't decline.
Traditional Letter Excerpt:

존경하는 어르신께,

날씨가 점점 추워지오. 건강히 지내시는지 궁금하오.

다음 달에 뵙기를 기대하오. 그때까지 평안하시길 빌겠소.

— 경손 올림

To the respected elder,
The weather is gradually getting colder. I wonder if you are well.
I look forward to seeing you next month. Until then, I wish you peace.
— Respectfully, Kyungson

Tone & Feeling

Polite but nostalgic, refined and historical. 하오체 evokes a sense of traditional Korean elegance and formality from previous generations. It sounds respectful yet slightly distant, carrying the weight of historical tradition. The tone is often described as "Confucian polite" - maintaining hierarchical respect while expressing politeness in a way that feels both formal and personal. While rarely used in modern everyday speech, it continues to appear in historical contexts, traditional ceremonies, and regional dialects, preserving Korea's linguistic heritage.
