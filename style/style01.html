Topic 1: 합니다체

Formal Polite / Deferential Speech

When to Use?

  • In news broadcasts and formal announcements
  • During presentations and public speeches
  • Military commands and official procedures
  • Customer service and business settings
  • Church sermons and religious ceremonies
  • With elderly people you don't know personally
  • Most distant or official speech level

Verb Endings

Type Ending Example English
Statement -ㅂ니다 / 습니다
갑니다
(go + 습니다)
 I go / (I'm) going
Question -ㅂ니까 / 습니까
갑니까?
(go + 습니까?)
 Do you go?
Command -(으)십시오
가십시오
(go + 십시오)
 Please go
Suggestion -(으)십시다
갑시다
(go + (으)십시다)
 Let's go

Examples in Context

Statement
저는 한국에서 일합니다.
I work in Korea.
Question
어디에서 오셨습니까?
Where are you from? (formal)
Narrative (Formal writing)
2025년 10월 1일에 회의가 진행되었습니다.
The meeting was held on October 1, 2025.
Conversation Example:
A
어디 갑니까?
Where are you going?
B
회의에 갑니다. 감사합니다.
I'm going to a meeting. Thank you.

Tone & Feeling

Extremely polite and respectful tone. This speech level maintains maximum social distance and shows the highest level of respect. It's used in all public announcements, formal documents, and situations requiring utmost politeness. The tone is professional, distant, and highly structured—perfect for situations where hierarchy and respect are paramount.
