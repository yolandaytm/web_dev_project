Topic 2: 해요체

Polite Informal / Standard Modern Speech

When to Use?

  • Everyday conversations with acquaintances
  • Speaking with strangers in public places
  • Classroom settings and teacher-student interactions
  • Customer service interactions (restaurants, shops)
  • Talking with colleagues at work
  • Meeting someone for the first time
  • Standard polite speech in modern Korean society

Verb Endings

Type Ending Example English
Statement -아요 / -어요 / -해요
가요 / 먹어요 / 해요
(go / eat / do + 요)
 I go / eat / do
Question -아요? / -어요?
가요? / 먹어요?
(go? / eat? + 요)
 Do you go? / Are you eating?
Command -(으)세요
가세요
(go + 세요)
 Please go
Suggestion -(으)ㄹ까요?
갈까요?
(go + ㄹ까요?)
 Shall we go?

Examples in Context

Statement
저는 학생이에요.
I am a student.
Question
집에 가요?
Are you going home?
Polite Command
기다리세요.
Please wait.
Suggestion
같이 먹을까요?
Shall we eat together?
Narrative (Storytelling)
어제 친구를 만났어요. 영화가 재미있었어요.
I met a friend yesterday. The movie was interesting.
Typical Conversation:
A
오늘 뭐 해요?
What are you doing today?
B
공부해요. 같이 카페 갈래요?
I'm studying. Do you want to go to a cafe together?
A
네, 좋아요! 몇 시에 만날까요?
Yes, sounds good! What time shall we meet?

Tone & Feeling

Neutral polite tone that sounds friendly and natural. This is the standard speech level used in most everyday conversations in modern Korean society. It maintains politeness while feeling approachable and comfortable. The tone is warm but respectful, making it perfect for conversations with people you're not extremely close to but want to maintain good relations with. It's commonly used in classrooms, customer service, and casual workplace interactions.
