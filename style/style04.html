Topic 4: 한다체

Plain / Narrative Form

When to Use?

  • News headlines and newspaper articles
  • Novels, books, and written literature
  • Movie and drama scripts
  • Personal diaries and journals
  • Talking to oneself or inner monologue
  • Teachers giving instructions to students
  • Reporters describing events
  • Scientific descriptions and reports
  • Older adults speaking to juniors in narration style

Verb Endings

Type Ending Example Meaning
Statement -ㄴ다 / -는다
간다 / 먹는다
(go + ㄴ다 / eat + 는다)
 (He) goes / (I) eat
Question -냐 / -니
가냐? / 먹니?
(go + 냐? / eat + 니?)
 Are you going?
Command -아라 / -어라 / -해라
가라 / 먹어라
(go + 라 / eat + 어라)
 Go / Eat
Suggestion -자
가자
(go + 자)
 Let's go

Examples in Context

Narration
새가 하늘을 난다.
A bird flies in the sky.
Storytelling / Writing
그는 조용히 문을 연다.
He quietly opens the door.
Teacher to Student
조용히 한다!
Be quiet! (Literally: "Do quietly!")
Diary Entry
오늘 일찍 일어났다.
I woke up early today.
News Headline
정부, 새로운 경제 정책 발표한다.
Government announces new economic policy.
Novel Excerpt:

어느 추운 겨울날, 한 소년이 길을 걷는다. 바람이 세차게 분다. 소년은 손을 주머니에 깊숙이 넣는다. 갑자기, 그는 멀리서 불빛을 발견한다. 집이다! 그는 서둘러 발걸음을 옮긴다.

On a cold winter day, a boy walks along the road. The wind blows fiercely. The boy puts his hands deep into his pockets. Suddenly, he spots a light in the distance. It's a house! He quickens his steps.
News Report Example:
대한민국, 올해 경제 성장률 3% 예상한다
전문가들은 한국 경제가 올해 3% 성장할 것으로 전망한다. 수출 호조와 내수 회복이 주요 동력으로 작용한다. 정부는 지속적인 성장을 위한 지원 정책을 마련한다.
Experts predict that the Korean economy will grow by 3% this year. Strong exports and domestic recovery act as main drivers. The government prepares support policies for sustained growth.

Tone & Feeling

Objective, descriptive, and neutral voice. 한다체 creates a sense of distance and objectivity, making it perfect for narration and formal writing. It's often called the "narrative form" because it's commonly used in storytelling, news reporting, and descriptive writing. The tone is factual and impersonal, focusing on describing actions and events rather than expressing personal feelings or addressing specific listeners. When used in speech (like teachers to students), it can sound authoritative and instructional.
